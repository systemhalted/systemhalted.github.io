For a decade it has maintained that its presence there is a fight against international terrorism. Afghan President Hamid Karzai's disclosure that the [United States is in talks with the Taliban](https://www.nytimes.com/2011/06/19/world/asia/19afghanistan.html) undercuts that claim, and the consequences could be severe for Afghanistan and for the region. The United States targeted the Taliban after the 9/11 attacks on the World Trade Center in New York. Since then it and its allies have occupied Afghanistan and repeated many times that this is a war against terrorism. Talks between an American delegation and associates of Mullah Omar put that stated purpose in question. The United States intends to withdraw its forces by 2014, and the drawdown is due to begin within a month. It appears to be in a hurry to settle the Afghan question, and if that means dealing with the Taliban, it is prepared to do so. The implicit offer is that the Taliban will be forgiven if it helps rebuild the country. The question is why the United States is negotiating with an organisation it went to war against. If the Taliban takes part in reconstruction, there is nothing to stop Afghanistan from returning to what it was before 2001. From an Indian perspective this is a setback to the work India has done on Afghan development. It helps Pakistan, and in particular the Inter-Services Intelligence, and it weakens the effort against terrorism in South Asia. The United States is preparing to hand the country back to the organisation it pledged to remove. Where is the morality in that, and what is the strategy?
