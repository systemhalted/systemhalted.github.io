#Copied from [Facebook Notes](https://www.facebook.com/note.php?note_id=10150158911256397)

Trying to analyze the verdict given by Hon Supreme Court on [Euthanasia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euthanasia "Euthanasia"). In my humble opinion, the court has done the right thing mainly because:-

1. [Art 21](http://rshrc.nic.in/07%20Human%20Right%20Article-21.pdf) of [Fundamental Rights](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fundamental_Rights_in_India) of [Indian Constitution](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_India "Constitution of India") that grants [Right to Life](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Right_to_life "Right to life") does not guarantee a [right to die](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Right_to_die "Right to die"). Even if there would have been any such right, then how a person who is in vegetative state exercise his or her right and how will his so-called well-wishers decide the will of that person.

2. We still need to work on infrastructure that is required for granting such a right.

While interacting with my friends on [Facebook](http://facebook.com "Facebook"),  Mr. Atul Lande, Director of Synergy Study Point Pune, suggested following:-

3. Supreme Court legalized passive euthanasia by means of the withdrawal of life support to patients in a [permanent vegetative state](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persistent_vegetative_state "Persistent vegetative state") (in reality this is already happening in India). Active euthanasia, including the administration of lethal compounds for the purpose of ending life, is still illegal in India (as there is no law related to this). SC has also provided guidelines for this. Law ministry wants a debate on the issue.

4. In India many religions and sects believe indirectly in passive euthanasia like in Jainism.

Any more points will be most welcome.
