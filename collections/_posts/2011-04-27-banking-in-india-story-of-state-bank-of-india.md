The oldest bank **in existence** in India is the State Bank of India, which originated in the **[Bank of Calcutta](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bank_of_Calcutta "Bank of Calcutta")** in June 1806, which almost immediately became the **Bank of Bengal**. This was one of the three presidency banks, the other two being the **[Bank of Bombay](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bank_of_Bombay "Bank of Bombay")** and the **[Bank of Madras](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bank_of_Madras "Bank of Madras")**, all three of which were established under charters from the [British East India Company](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_India_Company "East India Company"). For many years the Presidency banks acted as quasi-central banks, as did their successors. The three banks merged in**1921**to form the **[Imperial Bank of India](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_Bank_of_India "Imperial Bank of India")**, which, upon India's independence, became the **State Bank of India**.
